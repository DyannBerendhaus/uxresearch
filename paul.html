Honours project

Client

The client of this project is Creating 010. This is a Knowledge Center of Hogeschool Rotterdam that conducts research into social transformations associated with digitization and developments in information and communication technology.

Design problem

How can long-term sick students feel connected to the classroom through a robot?

Context

This project is from early 2020.

Long-term sick students must participate in school classes from a distance. They do not see their fellow students and teachers much, if at all. Because of this distance, students long for involvement with the class and teachers.

Role

Team lead, user experience researcher/designer

Publications

Operation

Approach

During my internship, I learned to work in a structured way. I wanted to impart this to my team, especially since we were working in and multidisciplinary team.

At the beginning of this collaboration, we made agreements as a team. In these I included not only standard agreements such as what to do when you are sick, but also agreements about how we envisioned this collaboration as a whole. This made it clear to us how we could guarantee the quality of our work and how communication would take place.

Structure

We started the day with a standup. Once a week a sprint planning meeting took place. Here we also discussed what we expected from deliverables (acceptance criteria). At the end of the week we held a retrospective to reflect on ourselves and our collaboration.

Discover

Problem statement

To conduct our research effectively, I suggested that we first create research questions using a word web. This made us first think about what the design question actually was. We also thought about what the purpose of the client's design question was.

Empathy map

By creating the empathy map, we discovered that we still had a few gaps in our research. One example is when is a student long-term sick. The empathy map (see right) actually served as a kind of summary of our focus group research up to that point.

Define

Values

  • Participation without limitations
  • Engagement
  • Tailor-made solution where needed
  • Independency
  • Self expression

Design criteria

  • Ability to show emotions
  • Ability to move
  • Ability to personalize
  • Wide angle lens to enable 360 streaming
  • Video streamen to the applicatie
  • Ability to move the head according to the gaze
  • Ability to move through the stream without delay
  • Audioconnection between the robot and the application

Design

Concept

By creating a concept sketch for each concept, others get a better idea of the concept than from a long description. This was useful to use in our concept presentation to the client.

Deliver

Paul

A 360-degree camera can film a much larger portion of its surroundings than a normal camera. This allows long-term sick students to navigate directly through the camera image without having to wait for the robot to turn. Thus, there is no delay for them, so they don't have to miss anything. The robot's head still rotates, so the people in the classroom know what the student is looking at.
